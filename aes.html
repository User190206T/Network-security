How AES Works

AES Process

  1. Key Expansion: Original key is expanded into multiple round keys.
  2. Initial Round: Plaintext is XORed with the first round key.
  3. Rounds: SubBytes (substitution), ShiftRows (permutation), MixColumns (mixing), and AddRoundKey (key addition).
  4. Final Round: Similar steps but without MixColumns.
  5. Output: Encrypted ciphertext.

Example

Plaintext: "HELLO"
Key: 128-bit key
Output: Ciphertext (encrypted unreadable text)

AES ensures confidentiality and makes it computationally infeasible to recover plaintext without the key.
